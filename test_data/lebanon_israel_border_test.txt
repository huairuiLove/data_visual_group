Title: Israel-Hezbollah Border Clashes Intensify, UNIFIL Warns of Wider War
Date: 2025-05-08
Source: Reuters

Summary:
Cross-border exchanges between Israel and Hezbollah escalate with casualties on both sides, as international mediators race to prevent a full-scale war in southern Lebanon.

Article:
BEIRUT/JERUSALEM — Hezbollah launched a barrage of 45 rockets and anti-tank guided missiles toward Israeli military positions in the disputed Shebaa Farms area on Wednesday, while Israeli warplanes struck Hezbollah positions in southern Lebanon, raising fears of a broader regional conflagration.

The exchange began when Hezbollah fighters targeted an IDF Merkava tank near the border village of Ghajar, killing two Israeli soldiers and wounding seven others. Hezbollah said the attack was in response to an Israeli drone strike hours earlier that killed three Hezbollah operatives in the town of Naqoura, near a UNIFIL observation post.

Israeli Defense Minister Yoav Gallant ordered retaliatory airstrikes against Hezbollah command centers, weapons depots, and rocket launch sites across southern Lebanon. Lebanese health authorities reported 12 killed and 35 wounded in the strikes, including paramedics from the Lebanese Red Cross who were responding to initial casualties.

Israeli Chief of Staff Herzi Halevi said the IDF was "prepared for a wider campaign in Lebanon if necessary" and had deployed two additional brigades — the 36th Armored Division and the Golani Brigade — to the northern border. Approximately 80,000 Israeli civilians remain evacuated from northern communities since October 2023.

Hezbollah Deputy Secretary-General Naim Qassem warned that the organization had "barely used 10% of its capabilities" and threatened to target the Israeli port city of Haifa and critical infrastructure including gas platforms in the Mediterranean Sea. Hezbollah is estimated to possess over 150,000 rockets and missiles, including precision-guided munitions capable of striking deep inside Israel.

UNIFIL, the UN peacekeeping force in southern Lebanon, said its headquarters in Naqoura was hit by what appeared to be an Israeli artillery shell, injuring two peacekeepers. UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres condemned the attack and called for an "immediate de-escalation."

French President Emmanuel Macron, whose country has historic ties to Lebanon, dispatched Foreign Minister Jean-Noel Barrot to both Beirut and Jerusalem for emergency diplomatic talks. France and the United States have reportedly drafted a proposal for a 21-day ceasefire and the establishment of a demilitarized zone extending 10 kilometers north of the Litani River, to be enforced by an expanded UNIFIL force.

The Lebanese government, currently without a president due to parliamentary deadlock, has struggled to assert control over Hezbollah's military operations. Caretaker Prime Minister Najib Mikati called for restraint but acknowledged the state's limited influence over Hezbollah's armed wing.

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi visited Beirut last week, meeting with Lebanese officials and Hezbollah leaders, and stated that "any Israeli aggression against Lebanon will have consequences for the entire region." The IRGC Quds Force commander Esmail Ghaani was also reported to have held consultations in Beirut.

In a related development, Israel's Iron Dome and David's Sling air defense systems intercepted 12 of the 45 projectiles fired by Hezbollah, with the remaining rockets falling in open areas or causing minor damage. Israel's emergency services treated 18 civilians for panic attacks and minor injuries from falling debris.

International airlines including Lufthansa, Air France, and Emirates have suspended flights to Beirut and Tel Aviv, while cruise lines have canceled Mediterranean port calls in the region. The US State Department raised its travel advisory for Lebanon to Level 4: Do Not Travel.

The World Bank estimated that a full-scale Israel-Lebanon war could cost Lebanon $12 billion in economic losses and destroy 15 years of development gains in an already crisis-stricken country facing hyperinflation, banking collapse, and 80% poverty rates.
