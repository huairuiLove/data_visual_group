Title: Gaza Ceasefire Talks Resume in Cairo Amid Renewed Violence
Date: 2025-03-15
Source: Al Jazeera

Summary:
Egypt, Qatar, and US mediators push for a three-phase ceasefire deal between Israel and Hamas as fighting intensifies in northern Gaza.

Article:
CAIRO — Ceasefire negotiations between Israel and Hamas resumed in Cairo on Tuesday, mediated by Egypt, Qatar, and the United States. The talks aim to establish a three-phase truce that would include the release of Israeli hostages held in Gaza and Palestinian prisoners in Israeli jails.

Hamas delegation arrived in Cairo late Monday, led by senior political official Khalil al-Hayya. The Israeli delegation, headed by Mossad Director David Barnea, joined via video link from Tel Aviv. Egyptian intelligence chief Abbas Kamel hosted the opening session.

Phase one of the proposed deal would see a 42-day pause in fighting, during which Hamas would release 33 Israeli hostages in exchange for approximately 900 Palestinian prisoners. Israeli forces would partially withdraw from populated areas in Gaza while maintaining positions along the Philadelphi Corridor on the Egypt-Gaza border.

Phase two involves a permanent ceasefire declaration and complete Israeli military withdrawal from Gaza, alongside the release of all remaining living hostages. Phase three would launch a major reconstruction effort for Gaza, estimated by the World Bank to cost over $60 billion.

Complicating the talks, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu reiterated demands that Israel retain security control over the Philadelphi Corridor and that Hamas must be completely disarmed. Hamas rejected both conditions, insisting on full Israeli withdrawal and the right to maintain armed resistance.

Meanwhile, violence continued on the ground. The IDF launched airstrikes on what it described as "Hamas command centers" in Jabalia refugee camp, killing at least 28 Palestinians according to Gaza health officials. In southern Israel, rockets fired from Gaza toward the city of Sderot wounded three Israeli civilians.

The United Nations Relief and Works Agency (UNRWA) warned of a catastrophic humanitarian situation in northern Gaza, where approximately 300,000 civilians face acute food shortages. The World Food Programme reported that only 15 aid trucks entered Gaza on Monday, far below the pre-war average of 500 per day.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Washington was "cautiously optimistic" about the Cairo talks but warned that "patience is running out." Qatar's Foreign Ministry emphasized that "the only path to regional stability runs through a just resolution of the Palestinian question."

The conflict, which began on October 7, 2023 after a Hamas-led attack on southern Israel killed 1,200 people, has resulted in over 42,000 Palestinian deaths according to the Gaza Health Ministry. More than 1.9 million Gazans have been internally displaced.

International Criminal Court prosecutor Karim Khan has sought arrest warrants for Netanyahu, Israeli Defense Minister Yoav Gallant, and three Hamas leaders for alleged war crimes and crimes against humanity. Both Israel and Hamas have rejected the allegations.

Regional powers including Saudi Arabia and the UAE have signaled willingness to contribute peacekeeping forces and reconstruction funding if a two-state solution framework is established. The Arab League has proposed a $20 billion Gaza reconstruction fund conditional on progress toward Palestinian statehood.
